Information About Yoga

Taught in the Iyengar tradition, all of these classes rely on an approach to classic Hatha Yoga that emphasizes correct alignment of the body in each asana (posture). Every week a different sequence is thoughtfully designed to help students focus on a specific category of asanas, such as standing poses, forward extensions, etc. The last week of the month is usually devoted to a slower, quieter restorative practice, and often includes pranayama (yoga breathing techniques).

Yoga practitioners enjoy many benefits from their efforts, among these are:

  • Stronger, better-toned muscles
  • Increased joint flexibility
  • Better balance and stability
  • Improved posture
  • Greater mind/body awareness
  • Reduced stress

Gentel Yoga

The Gentle Yoga class is appropriate for beginners, as well as those who have past or current experience with yoga. It includes some chair work, as well as basic standing poses (asanas). Students are encouraged to work at their own pace, textwithin the parameters of their own abilities and limitations. Props such as belts, blocks, blankets, bolsters and chairs are used to help participants work in specific poses that may be particularly challenging for them..

Yoga for 50+

This is a Level 1-2 class designed for students who are 50 years or older. We work on a full range of asanas, using props where needed to modify the classic versions of poses to make them safe and accessible for all participants. This class gives special consideration to specific asanas that help prevent and treat osteopenia and osteoporosis, based on the work of Dr. Loren Fishman.

Community Wellness Program

This is a basic Level 1 class that focuses on standing, seated and reclining asanas. It is suitable for beginners and those with some previous exposure to yoga. Access to props is somewhat limited – students are asked to bring their own yoga sticky mat to class. And, without blankets to use, we cannot practice shoulderstand (salamba sarvangasana), a pose that is typically part of the scope of a Level 1 class.

Private Lessons

Private yoga lessons are valuable and appropriate for a variety of reasons by offering individualized instruction to: support healing from injury or illness kick-start, guide or deepen home practice work on a specific asana or group of asanas provide a safe learning space for those uncomfortable in group classes

We can schedule private lessons in my home studio (in the Millsmont neighborhood of East Oakland); in your home; or at Adeline Yoga Studio (depending of space availability). The cost for a 1-hour session is:
$75 in my home studio
$85 or more in your home, depending on travel time and distance
$90 at Adeline Yoga Studio
Please contact me at sarah.harvey.yoga so we can discuss your needs and intentions regarding private yoga lessons.
